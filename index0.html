7 Ancient wonders of the world

Introduction

The 7 ancient wonders of the world are a list of 7 amazing structures that were built in the ancient world.
The list was compiled by ancient Greek historians and is a testament to the amazing engineering and architectural skills of the ancient world.
The list includes the Great Pyramid of Giza, the Hanging Gardens of Babylon, the Statue of Zeus at Olympia, the Temple of Artemis at Ephesus,
the Mausoleum at Halicarnassus, the Colossus of Rhodes, and the Lighthouse of Alexandria.

The Great Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza is the oldest and largest of the 3 pyramids in the Giza pyramid complex. It is also the only one of the 7 ancient wonders of the world that is still standing today.

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon were a series of terraced gardens built by the Babylonians. They were one of the most amazing engineering feats of the ancient world.

The Statue of Zeus at Olympia

The Statue of Zeus at Olympia was a giant statue of the Greek god Zeus. It was built by the Greek sculptor Phidias and was one of the most famous statues of the ancient world.

The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus

The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus was a Greek temple dedicated to the goddess Artemis. It was one of the largest and most beautiful temples of the ancient world.

The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus

The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus was a tomb built for Mausolus, a satrap in the Persian Empire. It was one of the most beautiful tombs of the ancient world.

The Colossus of Rhodes

The Colossus of Rhodes was a giant statue of the Greek god Helios. It was built to celebrate the victory of the Rhodians over the Macedonians.

The Lighthouse of Alexandria

The Lighthouse of Alexandria was a giant lighthouse built on the island of Pharos in the Mediterranean Sea. It was one of the tallest lighthouses of the ancient world.
